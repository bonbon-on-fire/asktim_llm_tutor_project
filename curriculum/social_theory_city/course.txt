Social Theory and the City — MIT OpenCourseWare

This course explores how social theories of urban life relate to the city's architecture and spaces, grounded in classic and foundational writings on topics including the public realm, impersonality, crowds and density, surveillance and civility, spatial justice, and the segregation of difference. Using Boston as a visual laboratory, students develop theoretical arguments connecting the social and the physical, culminating in a term paper that mediates what is read with what has been observed.
